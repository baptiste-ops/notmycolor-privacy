This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how NotMyColor ("we", "us", "our", or "NotMyColor") collects, uses, discloses, retains, and otherwise processes your personal information when you use the NotMyColor mobile application, our website at notmycolor.app, and any related features (collectively, the "Services"). By creating an account, signing in, or using the Services, you agree to the practices described here.

NotMyColor is a personal-color-analysis service: we determine your Sci/ART 12-season color season from photos you submit, and we help you score garments against your personal color palette. The Services are operated for individual end users; we do not sell user data, do not run advertising networks, and do not use cross-app tracking SDKs.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Email address and, if you sign up with email and password, the password you choose. The password is hashed by our authentication provider (Supabase); we never see, store, or transmit it in plaintext.
  • A unique user identifier (UUID) generated by Supabase to identify your account internally.
  • Sign in with Apple credentials when you choose this method (see Section 1.2).

1.2 Sign in with Apple

If you sign in with Apple, NotMyColor receives from Apple:

  • An identity token (JWT) and a SHA-256-hashed nonce used to verify your session with Supabase Auth.
  • An opaque Apple user identifier tied to your Apple ID and our app.
  • Your email address (either your real address or Apple's private relay address, depending on your choice).
  • Your full name, only on the very first authorization for this app. Apple does not return your name on subsequent sign-ins. If you provide a name, we store it in your profile as your first name.

We never receive your Apple ID password. You can manage or revoke this authentication at any time under iOS Settings → Apple Account → Sign in with Apple.

1.3 Profile and questionnaire

During onboarding we ask you to provide (some fields are optional):

  • First name
  • Age range
  • Gender (woman, man, non-binary, prefer not to say)
  • Your style vibe and clothing preferences
  • Your willingness to receive frank feedback
  • Your monthly clothing budget bracket
  • What you would like to change about your wardrobe
  • Your most honest reason for using the app (e.g., confidence, smarter shopping, getting compliments)
  • Self-described physical traits used for color analysis: hair color, eye color, skin reaction to sun
  • Wrist undertone (warm, cool, neutral) — either selected by you or inferred from a wrist photo

This information is stored in your account in our database (Supabase) and is used to personalize the color analysis, the garment-scoring engine, and the in-app experience.

1.4 Photos for color analysis (onboarding)

During onboarding you may capture or import:

  • A photo of your face (front camera, taken silently at the end of the face step).
  • An optional photo of your wrist (to determine your undertone).
  • Up to five additional selfies imported from your photo library.

These images are transmitted to OpenAI's API (United States) via our Supabase Edge Function proxy for the sole purpose of determining your color season and undertone. They are not stored on our servers and are not associated with your profile after analysis — only the resulting color season slug, runner-up season, and undertone are saved to your profile. See Sections 3 and 6 for details on AI processing.

1.5 Garment scan photos

When you scan a garment with the in-app camera or import one from your photo library, the photo is:

  • transmitted to OpenAI for color and garment-type analysis,
  • saved on your device under the app's local storage (in the app's Documents/scan_photos directory),
  • uploaded to your scan history in our database (Supabase, field scans.photo_url, JPEG compressed to ~600 px) so you can access your history across devices.

You can delete an individual scan or your entire history at any time from the Scans screen.

1.6 Profile picture (avatar)

If you set a profile picture from the Profile screen, the image is stored on your device and synchronized to your account row in our database (field profiles.avatar_url) so it can be displayed across sessions and devices. You can remove it at any time from the Profile screen.

1.7 Scan history and detected color metadata

For each scan we save:

  • Your detected color season and runner-up season
  • The dominant color of the garment (name, HEX code, Pantone reference when available)
  • A compatibility score and a "wear / depends / avoid" tag
  • The garment label and type (top, bottom, dress, etc.) inferred by AI
  • A short textual analysis generated by AI
  • Up to 3–5 alternative color suggestions ("runner-ups")
  • The timestamp of the scan

1.8 Color corrections and feedback

NotMyColor includes a feature that lets you correct the color detected on any scan. You can:

  • Confirm the detected color (thumbs-up), or
  • Open a picker to select the correct color from the AI-suggested alternatives ("runner-ups") or from the full color palette browser.

When you submit a correction, we record in the scan_corrections table: the scan identifier, your user identifier, the original color name and HEX, the corrected color name and HEX, the runner-ups that were offered to you, and the timestamp. Thumbs-up confirmations are also logged so we can measure model accuracy. See Section 5 for how this data improves the Services.

1.9 Subscription and purchase data

NotMyColor offers paid subscriptions (monthly and yearly) handled by Apple In-App Purchase (via StoreKit) and presented through our paywall partner Superwall. We do not see your credit card or bank details — those are handled entirely by Apple. We do receive:

  • Your subscription status (active, lapsed, trial, etc.) via Superwall and StoreKit, so we can unlock premium features.
  • The product identifier of the subscription you purchased (e.g., com.notmycolor.app.premium.yearly).
  • Anonymous paywall analytics from Superwall (paywall impressions, conversions, device identifiers managed by Superwall).

1.10 Free-trial usage counter

We store the number of scans you have performed (profiles.free_scan_count) so we can enforce free-trial limits before a paywall is presented.

1.11 Device and technical data

When you contact support from inside the app or this page, your email client may include the following diagnostic information so we can help you faster: your user ID, the app version and build, your iOS version, and your device model. NotMyColor does not collect IP addresses or device identifiers for advertising, and does not embed any third-party analytics, attribution, or crash-reporting SDK.

1.12 Local-only data

The following items are stored on your device only (in iOS UserDefaults or in the iOS Keychain) and are never sent to our servers:

  • App language, color scheme, notification preference, sound preference
  • Cached scan metadata and offline-queue items waiting to sync
  • Authentication tokens (Keychain: access token, refresh token)
  • Your locally cached profile picture

1.13 Local AI consent record

Before any photo is sent to our AI providers, you are asked to consent to AI processing. Your consent is recorded both on your device and on our server (profiles.ai_consent_at) so we can prove that processing took place on a lawful basis. You can withdraw this consent at any time by deleting your account (see Section 9).

3. Face Data

This section consolidates everything NotMyColor does with face-related data, in compliance with Apple's App Review Guidelines.

  • What we collect. Two distinct things: (i) on-device 2D facial landmarks (the position of your face, eyes, nose, mouth, and contour) computed live by Apple's Vision framework (VNDetectFaceLandmarksRequest) to align the on-screen face guide; and (ii) a single portrait JPEG of your face captured silently from the front camera at the end of the face step. We do not use ARKit, TrueDepth depth maps, 3D face meshes, biometric templates, or any face-recognition technology.
  • Why we collect it. Landmarks are used only to position the on-screen guide and detect whether your face is well-framed. The face JPEG, together with the optional wrist photo and up to five gallery selfies, is used exclusively to determine your Sci/ART 12-season color season.
  • Who receives it. Landmarks never leave your device. The face JPEG is sent to OpenAI (United States), via our Supabase Edge Function proxy, as part of a single gpt-4o chat-completion request.
  • How long it is kept. Landmarks are discarded as soon as the camera step closes (milliseconds to seconds). The face JPEG is not stored on our servers; only the resulting season slug is saved to your profile. OpenAI may retain the API request for up to 30 days for abuse monitoring, then deletes it.
  • How to revoke or delete. Submitting a face photo is part of the onboarding consent. You can delete your account at any time from the Profile tab, which wipes your profile, scan history, and authentication data from our database within 30 days. Because we do not retain face JPEGs, there is no face photo on our side to delete after the analysis completes.

5. AI Processing and Model Improvement

NotMyColor is an AI-first product: our color season analysis, garment scoring, and textual feedback are powered by AI models. This section explains in detail how those models work and how your data may be used to make them better.

5.1 What AI models we use today

  • Color season detection (onboarding). A single request to OpenAI's gpt-4o model, with your face selfie (and, optionally, your wrist photo and up to five gallery selfies), along with the questionnaire context (age, hair color, eye color, wrist undertone, style vibe).
  • Garment scan analysis. A request to gpt-4o with your garment photo plus a curated palette of approximately 300 reference colors. The model returns a color name, HEX value, dominant color analysis, and three to five runner-up alternatives.
  • Textual feedback and labels. Shorter requests to gpt-4o-mini generate the natural-language analysis displayed on each scan and the human-readable garment label (e.g., "navy crew-neck sweater").
  • Wrist photo validation. A request to gpt-4o-mini checks whether the wrist photo you submitted is usable (in focus, well-lit, actually shows a wrist) before being used for undertone analysis.

5.2 How AI calls are routed

All AI requests are sent from the app to a Supabase Edge Function proxy we operate, which then forwards them to https://api.openai.com/v1/chat/completions. The OpenAI API key is held server-side only and is never embedded in the app. Each request requires a valid Supabase session token, so anonymous users cannot trigger AI calls.

5.3 Using your data to improve our models

With your explicit consent given during onboarding, we may use your photos (face, wrist, additional selfies, garment scans), your scan history, your color corrections, your detected season, and your profile/questionnaire information to train, fine-tune, evaluate, and otherwise improve the AI models we use to provide the Services — including our own proprietary models and the third-party models we rely on — so that color analysis, garment scoring, and color-correction suggestions become more accurate, more diverse, and more inclusive over time.

Concretely, this means:

  • We may build internal datasets from the data above to evaluate model accuracy across skin tones, hair colors, eye colors, undertones, lighting conditions, and garment categories.
  • We may use your color corrections (the original color, the corrected color, the runner-ups offered) to retrain or post-train our matching pipelines and to tune the order in which we present alternative colors.
  • We may aggregate corrections across users — using a strictly anonymous statistical view (get_correction_stats) with a rolling 180-day window and a minimum threshold of 5 occurrences per color pair — to surface the most likely correct color when our primary detection is uncertain. This aggregated view does not contain your user identifier or any other personal data.
  • We may engage processors (including OpenAI, fine-tuning vendors, and labeling vendors) bound by confidentiality and data-protection contracts to perform these activities on our behalf.

Our primary color detection (the first step of our AI pipeline) is intentionally not biased by historical correction statistics, in order to avoid anchoring effects and to preserve the integrity of new detections.

5.4 Your control over AI processing

  • Consent. You give consent to AI processing during onboarding, before any photo leaves your device. Your consent (and its timestamp) is recorded locally and on our server (profiles.ai_consent_at).
  • Withdrawal. You can withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account (see Section 9). On deletion, your photos, scans, profile, and correction history are removed from our database, and we will no longer use that data to improve our models going forward. Deletion does not affect data already incorporated into model weights from prior training rounds, which by their nature cannot be selectively reversed; however, any future training rounds will exclude your data.
  • OpenAI training. According to OpenAI's published API data usage policy, data submitted through OpenAI's API is not used by OpenAI to train its public foundation models. OpenAI may retain API requests for up to 30 days for abuse and misuse monitoring, after which they are deleted, unless we activate Zero-Data-Retention for our account.

We share this data only with OpenAI and Supabase as described in Section 6, and never with advertisers, marketers, data brokers, or any other party for commercial purposes unrelated to the Services.

8. Data Retention

  • Account data (email, Apple identifier, profile, season, scan history, avatar, corrections, AI consent record): retained for as long as your account is active.
  • Onboarding analysis photos (face, wrist, additional selfies): not stored on our servers; may be retained by OpenAI for up to 30 days for abuse monitoring as described in Section 6.2.
  • Garment scan photos: retained in your scan history (Supabase) for as long as your account is active, and locally on your device until you delete the corresponding scan. Each scan photo is also transmitted to OpenAI, which may retain the API request for up to 30 days under its abuse-monitoring policy.
  • Color corrections: retained for as long as your account is active. After account deletion, individual rows linked to your user ID are removed (cascade delete). Aggregated, anonymous correction statistics that no longer identify you may be retained indefinitely for model improvement.
  • Authentication tokens: stored in iOS Keychain on your device; refreshed automatically and removed when you sign out or delete your account.
  • Local app data: removed immediately when you sign out or delete your account.
  • Subscription receipts: managed by Apple under Apple's own retention policies; we keep the minimum status flags needed to honor your subscription.

You can delete your account at any time from Profile → Delete account. On deletion, all personal data associated with your account in our database is permanently removed within 30 days. Local data on your device is removed immediately on sign-out or account deletion.

9. Account Deletion and Withdrawing Consent

You can delete your account at any time from the Profile tab inside the app. Deletion triggers a server-side Edge Function (delete-account) that:

  • Deletes all rows from the scans table that belong to you.
  • Deletes your row from the profiles table.
  • Deletes your authentication record from auth.users, which revokes your tokens and (via cascade) removes your scan_corrections.

The app then wipes all local data, including the Keychain tokens, the UserDefaults cache, the on-device scan photo folder, the avatar cache, and any pending offline-sync queues. Deletion is irreversible: we cannot restore the account.
