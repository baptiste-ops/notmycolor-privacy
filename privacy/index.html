This Privacy Policy explains how NotMyColor ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our iOS application. By creating an account or using NotMyColor, you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account information

When you create an account, we collect your email address and the password you choose. The password is hashed and stored by our authentication provider (Supabase); we never see or store it in plaintext.

1.2 Profile information

During onboarding we ask you to provide:

  • First name
  • Age range
  • Gender
  • Answers to a style questionnaire (typical reaction to a photo of yourself, willingness to receive frank feedback, monthly clothing budget, jewelry preference, what you would like to change about your wardrobe, your most honest reason for using the app, your style vibe)
  • Self-described physical traits used for color analysis: hair color, eye color, skin tone, wrist undertone

This information is stored in your account in our database (Supabase) and is used to personalize the analysis and the app experience. It is never sold to third parties or used for advertising.

1.3 Photos for color analysis

During onboarding you may take a photo of your face, a photo of your wrist, and optionally additional selfies. These images are transmitted to our AI partner (OpenAI) for the sole purpose of determining your color season and undertone (see Section 3). They are not stored on our own servers and are not associated with your profile after analysis.

1.4 Profile picture (avatar)

If you set a profile picture from the Profile screen, the image is stored on your device and synchronized to your account row in our database so it can be displayed across sessions and devices. You can remove it at any time from the Profile screen.

1.5 Garment scan photos

When you scan a garment with the in-app camera, the photo is:

  • transmitted to OpenAI for color and garment-type analysis (see Section 3),
  • saved on your device under the app's local storage,
  • uploaded to your scan history in our database (Supabase) so you can access your history across devices.

You can delete an individual scan or your entire history at any time from the Scans screen.

1.6 Color season and scan history

Your detected color season, runner-up season, compatibility scores, garment names/types, dominant colors, and the timestamp of each scan are stored in your account so you can access them across sessions.

1.7 Free-trial usage counter

We store the number of scans you have performed (currently capped while we finalize our pricing) so we can enforce free-trial limits.

1.8 Local preferences

The following preferences are stored on your device only (in iOS UserDefaults), never sent to our servers: app language, color scheme, notification preference, sound preference.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To create and authenticate your account.
  • To analyze your photos and determine your personal color season.
  • To score garments against your personal color palette and provide recommendations.
  • To display and synchronize your scan history across devices.
  • To enforce the free-trial scan limit and (in a future version) to manage subscriptions.
  • To respond to support requests sent to contact@notmycolor.app.
  • To detect abuse, fraud, or violations of our Terms of Use.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling for marketing, or sale to third parties. We do not currently use any analytics or crash-reporting SDK.

3. Third-Party Services and AI Processing

NotMyColor relies on two third-party processors:

3.1 Supabase (account, database, storage)

Supabase Inc. (United States) hosts our authentication system and our database. Your email, profile data, scan history, and avatar/scan photos are stored in our Supabase project. Supabase is bound by its own privacy policy and acts as our data processor under GDPR.

3.2 OpenAI (AI image analysis)

Color analysis is powered by OpenAI's GPT-4o vision models. Photos you submit (face, wrist, additional selfies, garment scans) are transmitted to OpenAI's API endpoints in the United States solely for the purpose of analysis. According to OpenAI's API data usage policy:

  • API inputs and outputs are not used to train OpenAI's models.
  • OpenAI may retain API requests for up to 30 days for abuse and misuse monitoring, after which they are deleted.

By using NotMyColor's analysis features you consent to this processing. If you do not consent, you can use the manual color-analysis path during onboarding, which never sends a photo off-device.

3.3 No other third parties

We currently do not use Firebase, Google Analytics, Mixpanel, RevenueCat, Superwall, push-notification services, advertising SDKs, or any tracking technology. If we add any such service in the future we will update this policy and notify you in the app.

3a. Face Data

This section consolidates everything NotMyColor does with face-related data so it is easy to find.

  • What we collect. Two distinct things: (i) on-device 2D facial landmarks (the position of your face, eyes, nose, mouth and contour) computed live by Apple's Vision framework (VNDetectFaceLandmarksRequest) to align the face guide circle while you take the photo; and (ii) a single portrait JPEG of your face captured silently from the front camera at the end of the face step. We do not use ARKit, TrueDepth depth maps, 3D face meshes, or any biometric template suitable for face recognition.
  • Why we collect it. The landmarks are used only to position the on-screen guide and detect whether your face is well-framed. The face JPEG, together with the optional wrist photo and up to five gallery selfies, is used exclusively to determine your Sci/ART 12-season color season.
  • Who receives it. The landmarks never leave your device. The face JPEG is sent to OpenAI (United States), via our Supabase Edge Function proxy, as part of a single gpt-4o chat-completion request.
  • How long it is kept. Landmarks are discarded as soon as the camera step closes (milliseconds to seconds). The face JPEG is not stored on our servers; only the resulting season slug is saved to your profile. OpenAI may keep the API request for up to 30 days for abuse monitoring, then deletes it.
  • How to revoke or delete. Submitting a face photo is part of the onboarding consent. You can delete your account at any time from the Profile tab, which wipes your profile, scan history and authentication data from our database within 30 days. Because we do not retain face JPEGs, there is no face photo on our side to delete after the analysis completes.

4. Sensitive Personal Data

Color season analysis involves processing visual characteristics of your appearance, including skin tone, hair color, eye color, and wrist undertone, as well as photos of your face and wrist. Under GDPR, photos that allow identification and information about physical characteristics may qualify as sensitive personal data. We process this information solely to provide the color-analysis service you requested, on the basis of your explicit consent given when you start the analysis. We share face and garment photos with OpenAI solely to deliver the color-analysis service described in Section 3.2, and with Supabase as our database processor. We do not share this data with advertisers, marketers, data brokers, or any other third party for commercial purposes.

5. Data Retention

  • Account data (email, profile, season, scan history, avatar): retained for as long as your account is active.
  • Analysis photos (face, wrist, additional selfies submitted during onboarding): not stored on our servers; may be retained by OpenAI for up to 30 days as described in Section 3.2.
  • Garment scan photos: retained in your scan history (Supabase) for as long as your account is active, and locally on your device until you delete the corresponding scan.
  • Garment scan photos sent to OpenAI: each scan photo is transmitted to OpenAI for color analysis. OpenAI may retain that API request for up to 30 days under its abuse-monitoring policy, then deletes it. Local thumbnails and Supabase scan history are independent and remain until you delete the scan or your account.
  • Authentication tokens: stored in iOS Keychain on your device; refreshed automatically and removed when you sign out.

You can delete your account at any time from Profile → Delete account. On deletion, all personal data associated with your account in our database is permanently removed within 30 days. Local data on your device is removed immediately on sign-out / account deletion.

6. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

  • Performance of a contract: account creation, authentication, color analysis, scan history.
  • Explicit consent: processing of photos and sensitive physical-trait information (Section 4). Consent can be withdrawn at any time by deleting your account or by contacting us.
  • Legitimate interest: abuse prevention and security.
